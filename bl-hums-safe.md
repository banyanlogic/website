# HUMS × SAFe — Signal Statements (Final)

*Each signal in you maps to a metric in SAFe. Read these aloud.*

-----

## B1 · WHO WE ARE

**S01 · Feel**
The Feel signal is like nothing yet in SAFe. eNPS and team sentiment surveys are the closest proxy — but they measure what people *report*, not what they *feel*. Over time, watch them alongside flow metrics as a culture signal, not a replacement for one.

**S02 · Ideate**
The Ideate signal is like the innovation % in Flow Distribution — how much of what’s in flight is actually new thinking.
The Ideate signal is like an OKR Objective — the aspiration before it becomes a number.
Use it to ask: is the ART making space for ideas, or just burning down the backlog?

**S03 · Remember**
The Remember signal is like ART Velocity tracked over time — the team’s memory of how much it has delivered, PI after PI.
The Remember signal is like Flow Velocity as a trend line, not a single reading.
The Remember signal is like Inspect & Adapt — the structured ritual where the ART encodes what it learned before moving on. Without I&A, the organism acts but does not remember.
You could see institutional memory degrading when velocity becomes erratic without a reason anyone can name, or when every PI Planning feels like starting over.

**S04 · Identify**
The Identify signal is like Value Stream KPIs — the metrics a stream chooses are the identity it declares.
Use it to ask: do the KPIs we track match who we say we are?

-----

## B2 · HOW WE WORK

**S05 · Think**
The Think signal is like OKR Key Results — deliberate, falsifiable reasoning about what matters.
The Think signal is like Flow Time — how long does it actually take to move a thought into the world?
The Think signal is like PI/Program Predictability — the quality of the forecast is the quality of the thinking.
Use it to assess whether decisions are being made against actual flow data or against instinct dressed as planning.

**S06 · Reflex**
The Reflex signal is like % Automated Tests — the proportion of quality verification that runs without being asked.
The Reflex signal is like Enabler Throughput — building the capacity to respond automatically, iteration by iteration.
Use it to gauge how much of the system’s response to quality problems has been encoded into the machine.

**S07 · Rely**
The Rely signal is like Flow Predictability — you can only rely on a system that does what it says.
The Rely signal is like PI Predictability — the heartbeat of trust across a Program Increment.
The Rely signal is like Story Throughput — consistency over time is what makes a team dependable, not peak performance.
Use it to assess forecast reliability and whether the pattern of delivery is one stakeholders can build on.

**S08 · Dream**
The Dream signal is like a Portfolio Vision — the long arc of where the organisation is pointed before any PI narrows it.
The Dream signal is like Strategic Themes — the guardrails that shape investment decisions across the portfolio.
The Dream signal is like an OKR Objective — the aspiration before the Key Results make it measurable.
Vision is an input to strategy, not a scored measure. The ambition of the dream goes unscored — and that is architecturally correct. What you can watch: whether Strategic Themes are genuinely directional or just last year’s initiatives relabelled.

-----

## B3 · SENSES (inputs)

**S09 · See**
The See signal is like Flow Load — the Kanban board is the literal visual field of the ART.
The See signal is like Flow Distribution — what the team sees when it looks at its work is the type-mix of what’s in flight.
Use it to ask: when we look at the board, are we seeing the full picture or just the urgent column?

**S10 · Hear**
The Hear signal is like NPS — delayed, aggregate, but a real signal from the customer’s voice.
The Hear signal is like Customer Experience Score — a reading of how the world responded to what we shipped.
The Hear signal is like First Response Time — how quickly the system hears and acknowledges a customer signal.
Use these as customer-outcome indicators tied to value streams, not as team performance grades.

**S11 · Smell**
The Smell signal is like Defect trend — a rising slope is the smell of something going wrong before it fully breaks.
The Smell signal is like MTTR — the longer it takes to recover, the worse the underlying smell.
Use it to sense system health early. By the time it shows on a dashboard, you should already have smelled it.

**S12 · Taste**
The Taste signal has no SAFe metric. Immediate satisfaction feedback at the moment of release — whether the work actually satisfies the person receiving it — is a structural gap in the framework. You could see it in demo reactions, in Sprint Review energy, in the first week of adoption. SAFe does not instrument it.

**S13 · Touch**
The Touch signal is like Flow Efficiency — the ratio of active to wait time is where you feel the friction in the system.
Use it to locate bottlenecks before they become crises. The friction is always there before the delay appears.

## B4 · HEALTH (processes)

**S14 · Breathe**
The Breathe signal is like Flow Efficiency — below 15% means the system is mostly holding its breath in wait states.
Use it to monitor rhythm. A healthy ART breathes steadily; a struggling one is gasping between approvals.

**S15 · Digest**
The Digest signal is like Feature Throughput — the rate at which value enters and is fully processed.
The Digest signal is like Story Throughput — the team-level digestive rate, iteration by iteration.
Use it to track whether value is moving through cleanly or accumulating in the gut of the system.

**S16 · Circulate**
The Circulate signal is like ART Velocity — the steady pulse of value moving through the release train.
The Circulate signal is like Flow Velocity — the heartbeat of the value stream.
Use it to understand train-level and value-stream throughput as a rhythm, not a race.

**S17 · Release**
The Release signal is like Flow Time at its exit point — the moment an item reaches the customer and the system lets it go.
The Release signal is like Defect resolution rate — clearing what the system has been carrying.
Use it to measure time-to-delivery and how quickly the system completes its cycles cleanly.

**S18 · Grow**
The Grow signal is like Enabler Throughput — every enabler completed is the system building new capacity, new muscle.
Use it to gauge the pace of capability-building that supports architectural runway. Growth you cannot measure is growth you cannot sustain.

**S19 · Age**
The Age signal is like KPI trend lines — not a single reading but the shape of performance across time.
The Age signal is like PI-over-PI Velocity — how the organism’s output changes as it matures.
Use it to assess performance direction. Over time you could see whether the ART is maturing or just persisting.

-----

## B5 · ACTIONS (outputs)

**S20 · Speak**
The Speak signal is like Flow Predictability — did the ART deliver what it said it would?
The Speak signal is like PI Objectives — the speech act of commitment at the start of each Program Increment.
Use it to reflect forecast reliability. The pattern of what the ART says versus what it does is the trust signal stakeholders actually read.

**S21 · Manipulate**
The Manipulate signal is like Unit Test Coverage % — the craftsperson’s hands shaping the codebase into reliable form.
The Manipulate signal is like # NFR Tests — hands-on verification that non-functional requirements hold under pressure.
The Manipulate signal is like Total Tests — the volume of the team’s craft output.
Use these to drive quality improvements and verify that what was built actually holds.

**S22 · Move**
The Move signal is like Flow Velocity — items in motion through the value stream.
The Move signal is like Feature Throughput — the locomotion of value at the feature level.
Use it to track the pace of delivery. Movement without direction is noise; movement traced to OKRs is signal.

**S23 · Eliminate**
The Eliminate signal is like the debt % in Flow Distribution — the system tracking what it needs to clear.
SAFe documents the existence of debt but does not prescribe a removal velocity metric. You can see the pile; you cannot see how fast you are letting go of it. That is the gap.

**S24 · Progeny**
The Progeny signal is like Revenue Impact KPIs — what the work leaves behind after the team has moved on.
The Progeny signal is like Customer Retention — the enduring relationship the product sustains.
The Progeny signal is like Outcome Metrics — the legacy of a delivery cycle measured in business impact.
Use these to assess what was actually left in the world, not just what was shipped.

-----

## B6 · PURPOSES (requirements)

**S25 · Live**
The Live signal is like Revenue Impact — the organisation’s will to continue, measured in survival currency.
The Live signal is like ART funding status — the starkest Live signal SAFe possesses: funded or defunded.
Use it as a portfolio-level health indicator. When this signal weakens, everything else becomes academic.

**S26 · Love**
The Love signal has no SAFe metric. The depth of care for the customer — whether the product reflects genuine understanding of their experience — goes unmeasured. NPS is the nearest proxy. It measures loyalty, not love. The gap is real and it is not an accident.

**S27 · Connect**
The Connect signal has no SAFe metric. The quality of belonging within and across teams — psychological safety, the felt sense of being part of something — is invisible to the framework. PI Planning is the mechanism SAFe provides. The quality of connection it produces is never scored. Over time, you could see it in how teams show up to planning. You cannot measure it there.

## B6 · Purposes — Fractal Signal: Team → ART → Portfolio

*HUMS is fractal. Two or more HUMS put together is still a HUMS. A team is a HUMS. An ART is a HUMS. A Portfolio is a HUMS. The B6 Purposes signals — Live, Love, Connect — do not change as you scale. What changes is the instrument that expresses them at each level. At team level they have no SAFe metric. At LPM level they fractal up as the quality filter behind Portfolio Kanban decisions. Strategic Themes are the formal LPM instrument; the B6 signals are what those Themes are protecting.*

**S25 · Live — fractals up to Epic Viability**
At team level, Live is the will to continue. At portfolio level, the same signal asks: does this Epic sustain the organisation’s ability to operate? An Epic that cannot answer that question is a Live signal failure dressed as a business case.
The signal is the same. The scale changed. Watch: are Epics being approved because they are viable, or because they are visible?

**S26 · Love — fractals up to Customer Value Hypothesis**
At team level, Love is the depth of care for the customer. At portfolio level, the same signal is the filter behind every Lean Business Case. Does this Epic reflect genuine understanding of what the customer needs, or is it a solution looking for a problem?
SAFe’s Lean Business Case asks for a customer value hypothesis. That hypothesis is the Love signal made operational at portfolio scale — imperfectly, but it is there.
Watch: are hypotheses being written to be tested, or to be approved?

**S27 · Connect — fractals up to Organisational Readiness**
At team level, Connect is the quality of belonging within a team. At portfolio level, the same signal asks: can the ARTs that must collaborate on this Epic actually work together? Is the dependency a coordination problem or a belonging problem?
Portfolio Kanban tracks dependencies. It does not track whether the teams on either end of that dependency trust each other enough to resolve them. That is the Connect gap at LPM level — the same gap, fractaled up.
Watch: when Epics stall, ask whether the blocker is a process gap or a connection gap.

-----

*Looking back, the signals that have no metric — Feel, Taste, Love, Connect — are not the soft ones. They are the ones the framework has not yet learned to see. At the LPM level, they are not absent — they fractal up into the quality of the decisions the Portfolio Kanban produces. You can see them in the choices. You cannot measure them in the metrics. That is what HUMS names that SAFe cannot yet see.*
